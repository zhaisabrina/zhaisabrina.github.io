VR Spaces: Feelings of Home

Visual / User experience Design, Spatial Design, Usability testing, User research
Exploration Space

Many people can feel unrooted in the place they live in. We examine curating feelings of home within a virtual reality space.

Our Response

We examine curating feelings of home within a virtual reality space by exploring culture, emotions, and rituals.

My role & contribution

As the designer on the team, I contributed to ideating and conceptualizing the space, immersing interactions in a VR world, and discussed findings from the research and how to incorporate it into the digital landscape.





01 / Affinity Diagram

As the design researcher, I primarily did research on feelings/concepts of home and practices in VR. Couples with the insights my team did as well, I created this affinity diagram and gathered four key insights.



Ritualistic interactions help build familiarity.



Customization allows ownership of space.



Feelings of comfort and easing of stress allows feelings of home to arise.



Continuous play fosters attachment.

02 / Storyboard

From the insights gathered from the affinity diagram, I began to ideate interactions within our space. I created storyboards in order to pitch different room ideas to the team before moving on to concept art.




02 / Floor Plan

After creating a couple rooms and testing out the exploration of this part, we began to tackle an actual house. After some discussion of which rooms to create (for example, the necessity of a kitchen vs bathroom in VR), I created a floorplan.
